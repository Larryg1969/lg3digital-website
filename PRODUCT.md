# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Small business owners in Wooster, Ohio and the surrounding Wayne County area. They run real businesses — shops, service firms, contractors, restaurants — and don't have an IT department or marketing team. They're busy, not technically savvy, and skeptical of agencies that overpromise. They want someone local they can trust to handle the digital stuff so they can focus on running their business.

## Product Purpose

LG3 Digital's marketing site exists to convert a small business owner's curiosity ("can someone help me with this?") into a booked consultation. It must immediately communicate that LG3 Digital is local, capable, and not an impersonal agency — then make it easy to reach out.

## Brand Personality

Energetic, bold, ambitious. LG3 Digital is genuinely excited about where AI is taking small business, and that energy should read in the design. Not frantic — directed. The personality is a local expert who leans forward in their chair, not a consultant who leans back.

## Anti-references

- **Big agency / corporate**: Polished to the point of cold. Generic "we help brands grow" language. Could be headquartered anywhere. Looks the same as 10,000 other agency sites.
- Overly minimal / Swiss: safe but forgettable. Energy needs to show.
- SaaS-cream aesthetic: warm neutral backgrounds, identical card grids, tracked all-caps eyebrows on every section.

## Design Principles

1. **Local confidence over corporate polish** — warmth and directness over slick distance. A person, not a brand system.
2. **Energy through typography, not noise** — boldness comes from type scale and weight contrast, not busy layouts or decoration.
3. **One clear next step** — every section should make the contact form feel inevitable, not one of many options.
4. **Substance over signals** — copy describes real outcomes ("your site won't go stale"), not capability theater ("end-to-end digital solutions").
5. **Honest scale** — a one-person shop is a feature, not a weakness. Own it.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Strong contrast on all body text, keyboard-navigable, screen reader friendly. General audience — no specialist needs identified.
